About Sharon

A life rebuilt. Not
from scratch, but
from memory.

Sharon Foulston is a grief and identity speaker who helps people recover the self they lost, not through crisis, but through the long, quiet years that followed one.

Her story is shaped by parental loss at the age of identity formation, the unexpected complexity of sudden wealth arriving alongside grief, and years of high-performing life that was nonetheless quietly hollow. The turning point came not in a therapist's chair. It came on the open sea.

"I was functional. But I wasn't alive. And the worst part is, I'd been that way so long I'd stopped noticing the difference."

Sharon's talks are drawn from lived experience, not theory. She speaks with the directness of someone who has been there and the warmth of someone who knows how long the quiet middle really lasts.

A signature methodology

The Return Framework™

Four stages that name what really happened, and what it takes to come back to yourself.

Stage 01

The Fracture

Identifying the original loss, along with the secondary losses it quietly created in the years that followed. Not just what happened, but what it took with it.

"Grief is not only losing someone. It is losing anything precious — a self, a future, a version of the life you thought you were building."

Stage 02

The Shell

Naming the long middle. The constructed self. The masks and distractions that kept life functional while keeping everything real at arm's length.

"I was functional. But I wasn't alive."

Stage 03

The Crack

The moment, or moments, that made ignoring the inner self impossible. Not always dramatic. Often quiet. Always undeniable.

"The moment it stops fitting is not a catastrophe. It is an invitation."

Stage 04

The Return

The active, deliberate work of coming back. Simplifying. Nature. Presence. One decision at a time.

"I didn't reinvent myself. I remembered myself. All it took was the sea, some silence, and the willingness to stop running."
